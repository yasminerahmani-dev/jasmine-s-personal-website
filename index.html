Making YouTube Videos

I have been making YouTube videos for longer than I would like to admit. At first, I created some Minecraft videos, then some tutorials on how to download other games and software. Then I moved to making video essays about some of my favorite games, tech-related content, and video game challenge runs. I am planning to make more video essays and some videos about my new passion for electronics. check out my channel if you want to see some of my work, and maybe subscribe if you like it.

Video Games

I started my gaming journey on the Xbox 360, where I played my favorite game ever, Bioshock. I am more of a single-player gamer, but I do enjoy playing with friends, mostly couch co-op games and sometimes online games like Rocket League and Fall Guys. However, I am not a fan of the battle royale genre ,you can call me a hater. I only play it with friends, not alone online. A year after I got my 360, I got an Xbox One S. There, I started playing more online games but realized it's not for me. I prefer playing with friends in person or just single-player games. Then I got my first PC, followed by a Nintendo Switch, a PS3, and a PS4. My latest addition to my gaming collection is a DS Lite.

Computers

I grew up with a family computer in the middle of our house and have always been interested in how they work and what they can do. My interest in video games led me to learn more about computers because I tried downloading some games that didn’t work. That was the moment I realized that my Intel Pentium 4GB RAM computer was not good enough, and I needed to upgrade it. So I started learning about computer hardware and how to build a PC. After a few years of learning and saving up, I built my first PC and it was a great experience… for a few days. Then problems started to appear because of the used, outdated hardware I had chosen. I had to learn how to troubleshoot and fix it, which turned into a great learning experience. Now, after years of watching builds online and binge-watching LTT videos, I finally have a more up-to-date machine that lets me play games and do video editing without problems.

Video Editing

I got into video editing a few years ago. It started as a way to make Minecraft videos, but it has become the skill I am most passionate about. I started with Filmora, then moved to Premiere Pro, and now I am trying to switch to DaVinci Resolve without much success yet. So I may just stick with Premiere Pro and After Effects for now.

Electronics

My interest in electronics started this year. I have been obsessed with random engineering YouTubers like The Backyard Scientist, Michael Reeves, and especially William Osman I binge-watched all his videos. He inspired me to get into electronics as a hobby because it can be fun, and it's no longer that scary thing only "smart people" can do. So I got myself an Arduino starter kit, and I am loving it. I have been learning a lot about how to use it and how to make cool projects, like a real-life video game health bar. I am looking forward to learning more and building even more projects.

3D Design

Pinterest and YouTube introduced me to 3D design. I have been fascinated by that PSX retro visual style and have been trying to recreate some of those designs in my own work using Blender and free online resources.
